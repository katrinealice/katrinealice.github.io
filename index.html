About me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the CMB&CO group at the Institute for Theoretical Astrophysics (ITA) at the University of Oslo, where I am a part of developing the Bayesian end-to-end framework Cosmoglobe. I was previously at University of Manchester (UoM) as a Post Doctoral Researcher, focusing on data and calibration of MeerKLASS. Before this, I was a PhD-student at UoM in 2021-2025 under supervision of Phil Bull. Here, my work focused on developing a Bayesian mapmaking formalism for 21cm interferometers, e.g. HERA. I did my MSc degree in Astrophysics in 2018-2020 at Aarhus University under supervision of Steen Hannestad and Thomas Tram in numerical Cosmology where I did forecasts for SKA using MontePython based on non-standard cosmological models, and my BSc (undergraduate) in Physics with Astrophysics in 2015-2018 also at Aarhus University.

Outside of research, I'm particaularily proud of having run the monthly forum EquiTea, at the Jodrell Bank Centre for Astrophysics (JBCA), creating a space for discussing Equity, Diversity and Inclusion. The sesssions was often led by invited speakers and has been a great space for open discussions and with positive feedback from both PhD students and staff.

Research

I am specifically interested in the early Universe and the Large Scale Structure of the Universe.

Currently, I work on Bayesian modelling and component separation primarily focusing on foregrounds to the CMB while also expanding into the infrared (CIB) and radio (21cm Epoch of Reionisation). I am also an active member of HERA and MeerKLASS amongst several other on-going projects such as RHINO, COBRA-X, and (naturally) Cosmoglobe and Commander.

During my PhD my main project has been to develop a Bayesian full-sky mapmaking formalism for 21cm interferometer visibilities from instruments such as (but not limited to) the Hydrogen Epoch of Reionization Array (HERA). Keywords would be: Gaussian constrained realisations, Gibbs sampling, EoR, diffuse foregrounds and spherical harmonics. In the process I have taken a special interest in investigating excess radio backgrounds such as the ARCADE2 and LWA results, which, besides being essential to eventually uncover the underlying 21-cm signal, also has the possibility to unlock new physics!

Other topics that have caught my interest are CMB B-mode polarisation and other LIM tracers of the early Universe and the EoR, especially in the context of joint analysis with 21-cm experiments.
